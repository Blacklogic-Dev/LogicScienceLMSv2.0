🔌 Electromagnetism

Weight: High

Electromagnetism studies electric currents and magnetic fields and their interactions. It includes the concept of electromagnetic induction.

⚡ Key Formula:

Faraday's Law:
EMF = -N × ΔΦ / Δt

💡 Tip:

Φ represents magnetic flux. EMF is the voltage generated.

🌊 Waves & Optics

Weight: Medium

This section covers light behavior, refraction, reflection, lenses, and wave properties like frequency and wavelength.

📐 Equation:

v = f × λ
Where:
v = speed of wave
f = frequency
λ = wavelength

💡 Tip:

Use Snell's Law for refraction: n₁sinθ₁ = n₂sinθ₂

☢ Nuclear Physics

Weight: High

Nuclear Physics explores the nucleus, radioactive decay, nuclear reactions, and energy released from fission and fusion.

🔬 Formula:

E = mc²
Where:
E = energy
m = mass
c = speed of light (3×10⁸ m/s)

💡 Tip:

Alpha (α), beta (β), and gamma (γ) are types of radiation.
