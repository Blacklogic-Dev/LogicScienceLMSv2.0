🧬 Cells

Weight: High

Cells are the basic units of life. All living organisms are made up of cells. There are two main types: prokaryotic and eukaryotic. Each cell contains structures called organelles, which perform specific functions.

🔍 Tip:

Key Organelles:
- Nucleus: controls activities
- Mitochondria: energy (ATP)
- Ribosomes: protein synthesis

💡 Note:

Plant cells have a cell wall and chloroplasts, which animal cells do not.

🌿 Photosynthesis

Weight: Medium

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight to make food. It takes place in the chloroplasts and requires sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water.

🌞 Equation Example:

6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
# Carbon dioxide + water → glucose + oxygen

💡 Tip:

This process is endothermic – it needs energy (light).

👶 Reproduction

Weight: Medium

Reproduction is the biological process of producing offspring. There are two types: asexual and sexual reproduction. Humans and most animals reproduce sexually.

🔗 Key Points:

- Asexual: 1 parent, identical offspring
- Sexual: 2 parents, genetic variation
- Meiosis: produces gametes (sperm and egg)

💡 Tip:

Meiosis reduces the chromosome number by half, ensuring offspring have the correct amount.
