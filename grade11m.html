🧮 Quadratic Equations

Weight: High

Quadratic equations are expressions in the form ax² + bx + c = 0. The solution is found using the quadratic formula or by factorising.

📐 Formula:

x = (-b ± √(b² - 4ac)) / 2a

💡 Tip:

"±" means there are two solutions: one with addition, one with subtraction.

📊 Functions and Graphs

Weight: Medium

Functions are rules that assign inputs to outputs. A graph visually represents the relationship. Examples include linear, quadratic, and exponential functions.

📉 Example:

# Linear Function
y = mx + c
# m = slope, c = y-intercept

💡 Tip:

The slope (m) tells us how steep the graph is. A negative slope means the line falls.

📐 Trigonometry

Weight: High

Trigonometry is the study of relationships between angles and sides of triangles. Common ratios include sine, cosine, and tangent.

🧠 Key Ratios:

sin(θ) = opposite / hypotenuse
cos(θ) = adjacent / hypotenuse
tan(θ) = opposite / adjacent

💡 Tip:

Use the acronym SOH-CAH-TOA to remember the trigonometric ratios easily.
