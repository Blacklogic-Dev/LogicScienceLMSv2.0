📘 Algebra Basics

Weight: Medium

Algebra introduces the concept of variables, expressions, and equations. It is used to solve problems by forming mathematical statements and simplifying them.

📐 Equation Example:

# Solve: 2x + 3 = 11
2x + 3 = 11
2x = 11 - 3
2x = 8
x = 8 / 2
x = 4

💡 Tip:

The * symbol (asterisk) is used in code or text as a multiplication sign. For example: 3 * 2 = 6.

📘 Linear Graphs

Weight: Medium

Linear graphs represent equations of the form y = mx + c. They are straight lines on a graph, where m is the slope (gradient) and c is the y-intercept.

📐 Equation Example:

# Graph of y = 2x + 1
# x: -2, -1, 0, 1, 2
# y: -3, -1, 1, 3, 5

💡 Tip:

The 'm' in y = mx + c is the slope, which tells us how steep the line is. A positive m means the line rises as x increases.

📘 Geometry

Weight: High

Geometry deals with shapes, angles, and the properties of space. You will learn about triangles, circles, and how to calculate area and perimeter.

📐 Equation Example:

# Area of a triangle: A = 1/2 * base * height
base = 6
height = 4
A = 0.5 * 6 * 4 = 12

💡 Tip:

Always write units in your final answer. For example: 12 cm² (for area).
